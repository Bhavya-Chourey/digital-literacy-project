# Case Study: UPI/Online Payment Fraud

**Cybercrime Type:** UPI/Online Payment Fraud  
**Scenario Type:** Realistic Fictional Scenario  

---

## What Is This Crime?

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a form of digital financial crime where attackers deceive victims into authorizing fraudulent transactions or revealing sensitive credentials through psychological manipulation. It is one of the fastest-growing cybercrime categories in India due to the widespread adoption of apps like Google Pay, PhonePe, and Paytm.

---

## How It Typically Happens (Step-by-Step)

1. **Target Selection:** The attacker identifies a victim — often someone who has recently posted about selling an item on OLX, Facebook Marketplace, or similar platforms.
2. **Initial Contact:** The fraudster contacts the victim posing as a genuine buyer, often using a fake military or government identity to build trust.
3. **The Deception:** The scammer says, *"I'll send you an advance payment via UPI. Please scan this QR code / click this link / enter your UPI PIN to receive the money."*
4. **The Trap:** The victim scans a **collect request** QR code or clicks a spoofed UPI link — which actually *debits* money from their account instead of crediting it.
5. **Money Lost:** The moment the victim enters their UPI PIN, the money is transferred to the attacker's account, often routed through multiple mule accounts to avoid tracing.
6. **Disappearance:** The fraudster immediately blocks the victim and vanishes.

---

## Who Is Usually Targeted?

- College students and first-time UPI users unfamiliar with how "collect requests" work
- People selling goods on second-hand marketplaces
- Small shopkeepers and daily wage workers
- Senior citizens new to digital payments
- Job seekers offered fake "registration fee refunds"

---

## Consequences

- **Financial:** Victims lose money instantly with little chance of recovery, as UPI transactions are irreversible once authorized.
- **Psychological:** Victims experience shame, anxiety, and distrust of digital payment systems.
- **Academic/Career Impact:** For students, losing funds meant for fees or essentials creates serious hardship.
- **Legal Complexity:** Tracing funds is difficult as fraudsters use multiple intermediary bank accounts.

In one such realistic scenario, a first-year college student in Madhya Pradesh lost ₹8,500 — money saved for semester books — after scanning a fraudulent QR code sent by a fake buyer for a second-hand laptop listed online.
