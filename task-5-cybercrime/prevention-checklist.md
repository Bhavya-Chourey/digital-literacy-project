# 🛡️ Stay Safe Online – Prevention Checklist
### For College Students in India

Use this checklist to protect yourself from cybercrime, especially UPI fraud, phishing, and identity theft.

---

## ✅ General Online Safety

| # | Prevention Tip | Why It Matters |
|---|---------------|----------------|
| 1 | **Never share your OTP, UPI PIN, CVV, or bank password with anyone** — not even someone claiming to be from your bank or government. | Legitimate institutions never ask for these details. Sharing them gives attackers full access to your account. |
| 2 | **Use strong, unique passwords for every account** and enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) wherever available. | Reusing passwords means one data breach can compromise all your accounts. |
| 3 | **Verify links before clicking** — check that URLs start with `https://` and match the official website address exactly. Avoid shortened URLs from unknown senders. | Phishing links mimic real websites to steal your login credentials. |
| 4 | **Keep your apps, operating system, and antivirus updated regularly.** | Updates patch security vulnerabilities that hackers actively exploit. |
| 5 | **Avoid using public Wi-Fi for banking, UPI transactions, or logging into sensitive accounts.** Use mobile data or a trusted VPN instead. | Public Wi-Fi networks can be monitored by attackers to intercept your data. |
| 6 | **Review app permissions before installing** — deny access to contacts, camera, or location unless absolutely necessary for the app's function. | Malicious apps harvest personal data by requesting excessive permissions. |

---

## 💳 Financial / UPI-Specific Safety (Mandatory Tips)

| # | Prevention Tip | Why It Matters |
|---|---------------|----------------|
| 7 | **Understand the difference between "Send" and "Receive" in UPI** — you should NEVER need to enter your UPI PIN or scan a QR code to *receive* money. If someone asks you to do this, it is a scam. | This is the #1 trick used in UPI fraud. Entering your PIN on a collect request sends money *out* of your account. |
| 8 | **Set a daily UPI transaction limit** in your banking app and enable SMS/email alerts for every transaction. | A transaction limit reduces maximum possible loss, and instant alerts help you detect fraud immediately and report it. |

---

## 🎓 College Student-Specific Tips

| # | Prevention Tip | Why It Matters |
|---|---------------|----------------|
| 9 | **Be cautious of "job offers," "scholarship links," or "fee refund" messages** sent via WhatsApp, Instagram DMs, or email that ask you to click a link or pay a small fee first. | These are common traps targeting students desperate for income or financial aid. |
| 10 | **Never post photos of your ID cards, marksheets, or Aadhaar card publicly on social media.** | Personal documents can be misused for identity theft, SIM swapping, or taking loans in your name. |

---

## 🚨 If You Are a Victim – Act Immediately

1. **Call Helpline 1930** (National Cyber Crime Helpline) as soon as possible — quick reporting can freeze fraudulent transactions.
2. **File a complaint online** at 🔗 [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in)
3. **Inform your bank** immediately to block your card/UPI and request a transaction reversal.
4. **Take screenshots** of all conversations, transaction IDs, and suspicious numbers as evidence.
