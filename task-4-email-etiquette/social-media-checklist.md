# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

A checklist for responsible and professional social media use during your college years.

---

## ✅ DO's

| # | Do | Why It Matters |
|---|-----|----------------|
| 1 | **Think before you post** – Ask yourself: "Would I be comfortable if a future employer or professor saw this?" | Your digital footprint can follow you for years and affect internships, jobs, and opportunities. |
| 2 | **Keep your profiles updated and professional** – Use a clear profile photo and accurate bio on platforms like LinkedIn. | A polished profile builds credibility and helps you network effectively. |
| 3 | **Use privacy settings wisely** – Regularly review who can see your posts, photos, and personal info. | Protecting your personal data reduces the risk of identity theft and unwanted exposure. |
| 4 | **Give credit when sharing others' content** – Tag original creators and cite sources when reposting. | Respecting intellectual property is both ethical and legally important. |
| 5 | **Engage positively and constructively** – Support peers, share helpful resources, and participate in meaningful discussions. | Positive engagement builds your reputation and contributes to a healthy online community. |
| 6 | **Log out of shared or public devices** – Always sign out of accounts when using college labs or shared computers. | Leaving accounts open on shared devices can lead to unauthorized access or misuse. |

---

## ❌ DON'Ts

| # | Don't | Why It Matters |
|---|-------|----------------|
| 1 | **Don't post offensive, discriminatory, or hateful content** – Avoid language or images that demean others based on race, gender, religion, or identity. | Such content can result in disciplinary action, social harm, and lasting reputational damage. |
| 2 | **Don't overshare personal information** – Avoid posting your phone number, home address, class schedule, or financial details publicly. | Oversharing can make you a target for scams, stalking, or identity theft. |
| 3 | **Don't engage in cyberbullying or online harassment** – Never send threatening messages, spread rumors, or mob others online. | Cyberbullying causes serious emotional harm and may have legal consequences. |
| 4 | **Don't plagiarize or misrepresent others' work** – Don't screenshot and repost without credit, or claim others' ideas as your own. | Academic integrity applies online too; violations can lead to serious academic penalties. |
| 5 | **Don't post in the heat of the moment** – Avoid venting frustrations about professors, classmates, or institutions impulsively. | Emotional posts are hard to take back and can damage relationships and your reputation. |
| 6 | **Don't neglect your mental health** – Avoid excessive scrolling, comparing yourself to others, or seeking validation through likes. | Excessive social media use is linked to anxiety, poor sleep, and reduced academic performance. |
