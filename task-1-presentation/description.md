# Task 1 – Digital Literacy Infographic: Project Report

## Tool Used
I designed this infographic using **HTML, CSS, and Google Fonts** — essentially treating a web browser as my canvas. This approach gives complete creative control over typography, layout, color, and spacing without being limited by a template.

## What the Design Shows
The infographic is a single-page visual resource structured around five key areas of digital literacy. It opens with a bold typographic headline to immediately catch attention, followed by a dark definition block that answers *what digital literacy is* and its five core pillars. Three color-coded cards then cover the most student-relevant topics: useful digital tools (Google Docs, Canva, Notion, Zotero), safe internet practices (strong passwords, phishing awareness, VPNs), and email etiquette (subject lines, greetings, proofreading). A purple card addresses professional online presence — LinkedIn, portfolio sites, and social media auditing. A contrasting red statistics card reinforces *why* all of this matters with real-world data. A call-to-action strip at the bottom encourages immediate small steps.

## One Interesting Challenge
The most interesting challenge was **visual hierarchy** — deciding which information needed to stand out most. Using contrasting background colors for each card made the sections scannable, but balancing that with a cohesive overall palette required multiple iterations to avoid the design feeling chaotic.
